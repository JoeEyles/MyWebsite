Personal projects

SpotAJoe: A music streaming service for my family. A Node.js server is running on a Raspberry Pi, with a database of music to draw from. My router is configured to allow http requests through, so that the Pi can be accessed from the external internet. A link to the Raspberry Pi's ip address can be found here, however it is probably offline at the moment.


Campervan trip: a map of our campervan trip in 2018. I used Open Street Map and Leaflet.js for the map itself. The route is taken from Google location data. The images are placed on the map using their meta data (either using the location or the time). You can find it here.


Moving cities (working title): under construction (currently on hold), a desktop and Android game in which you direct your city around a mysterious steam punk world, all the while gathering resources and building on your city. This is being written in Java, using LibGDX. I am using blender to create the 3D models, and GIMP for the artwork.


Curve planetoid: an Android game in which you pit your 3D thinking skills against this space distorting game. Aim and curve your shots over planetoid surfaces to hit the target. This app makes a folded 2D surface resting in 3D space out of a triangular mesh. Player and target positions are shown, and the player can shoot in any direction. The curving shot wraps around the surface (creating a geodesic route), and possibly hits the target! You can find a paid version here, and a free version here. This was written in Java, using LibGDX. I wrote the mesh and surface generation software myself.


Tree Sim: this relaxing Android app lets you craft your perfect tree in a peaceful environment. The beautiful environment creates the perfect backdrop for your fully 3D tree. You can find it for free here. Again, this was written in Java, using LibGDX. It relies heavily on shaders, which I wrote myself.


Pool of Blood: a murder mystery game for Android. Betsy Walter, a notable journalist, has been brutally killed! The suspects include her brother, her husband, and a mysterious figure, caught on CCTV leaving the crime scene. Hear the intriguing profiles on each suspect, and then zoom into the carefully crafted crime scene photos to find the clues. Once you have gathered all of the evidence, brand one of the suspects as guilty! You can find it for free here. This was written in Java, directly for Android.

Older projects

Rokolector: a simple Android game based on Asteroids. I wrote it in html5/javascript, because I thought it would be fastest, and my goal was releasing a game, as opposed to the game itself.

Cubic Interpolator: this game originates with me doing some idle maths in order to join a number of points together with a smooth curve. I solved it first with a series of quadratic functions, but the result tended to oscillate wildly. I then solved it with a series of cubic functions, constraining the turning points to be on the points. This created a much smoother effect, and inspired me to begin creating this simple side scrolling game. You fly a space ship through zones killing enemies and collecting powerups. The zones are randomly generated, but deterministic, and your current zone is saved between plays. Here the interface is native to android, but the game itself runs on a html5 canvas in a WebView.

Genetic Brains: this program is very much a work in progress. The creatures travel the world eating, avoiding dangers, and reproducing. They are controlled by unique neural nets, that evolve as they reproduce. It is written in C#, using OpenTK for the simple graphics.
